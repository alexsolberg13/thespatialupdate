The Strait of Hormuz: Oil's Most Critical Passage

Reference map  ·  Updated March 2026

Every day, roughly 21 million barrels of oil — about one fifth of global supply — pass through a corridor barely 3 kilometres wide at its narrowest point. The Strait of Hormuz is the single most important chokepoint in the world's energy system.

Iran's coastline runs along the entire northern shore. Any conflict involving Iran immediately raises the question of whether — and how — traffic through the Strait might be disrupted.

Iran has periodically threatened to close the Strait — most recently during the 2019 maximum pressure campaign and again following the 2025 strikes. While outright closure has never occurred, tanker seizures and attacks on shipping have happened repeatedly.
