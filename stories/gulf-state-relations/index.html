The Middle East's geopolitical landscape is defined by a fundamental tension between Iran and a coalition of Arab Gulf states, with the United States as the dominant external power and a network of proxies extending the conflict across the wider region.

Iran's strategy relies on what it calls the Axis of Resistance — a network of allied militias and political movements spread across the region that allows Iran to project power without direct military engagement. Each node in this network is a separate pressure point that Iran can activate or restrain depending on the diplomatic situation.

The Gulf states' response has been to deepen security ties with the United States while pursuing parallel diplomatic normalisation with Israel — a realignment that would have been unthinkable a decade ago.
